Step by Step

The Adoption Process

01

Browse & Reach Out

Browse available breeds above or contact Brooke at (260) 443-9035 about upcoming litters and waitlists.

02

Place a Deposit

A $200–$400 nonrefundable but transferable deposit holds your spot. Zelle, CashApp, or Cash. Tell us your preferred gender.

03

Pick Your Puppy

Virtual picks begin at 3–4 weeks for solid litters. Multicolor picks begin at 1–2 weeks via FaceTime. In-person visits at 6 weeks.

04

Submit Application

Complete the puppy application before your visit. This helps us ensure the perfect match for our fur-babies.

05

Welcome Home!

At 8 weeks your puppy comes home with a goody bag of toys, food, a scent blanket, vaccine records, and health guarantee. Balance due in cash.

Our Story

A Family Farm Built on Love

Yarnelle Fur-Babies is a small, family-run breeding program on a beautiful 10-acre farm in Columbia City, Indiana — with 6 acres of in-ground fencing so our dogs can run and play every single day.

John and Kathy Yarnelle have been raising equine livestock for over 35 years through Yarnelle Farms Equine, LLC. As their family grew — through biological children, adoptions from Guatemala, and an ongoing journey adopting from Haiti — so did their love of raising exceptional dogs and doodles.

All puppies are handled with love daily and socialized with children of all ages (newborn through 16!), other dogs, and cats. Every pup deserves individual attention, care, and affection — and that's exactly what they get.

Questions & Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before bringing your new fur-baby home.

Our puppies are family-raised around children from newborn to 16, as well as other dogs and cats, every single day. Only our family handles them for the first 6 weeks to protect against illness. In-person visits begin at 6 weeks after first vaccinations — by appointment only.
We use Purina Puppy Chow Tender & Crunchy. We'll send a bag home with your puppy. If switching foods, transition gradually over 7–10 days to avoid stomach upset.
Transition stress is completely normal. Puppies are leaving their mom, siblings, and everything familiar. Plain white rice or pure pumpkin mixed into food helps firm up their stool. Remember — all dogs need deworming monthly throughout their lives.
The first 8 weeks are critical for behavioral development — puppies learn vital social skills from their mom and littermates. It is also Indiana state law. We're flexible about scheduling once the 8-week milestone is reached.
Puppies' bladders aren't fully developed until 4–6 months, so expect bathroom trips every 1–3 hours. We introduce a separate potty area at 3 weeks. We recommend crate training from day one with a divider. Many families love the "bell method" — easy to find on YouTube.
A goody bag with their favorite toys, food for the first few days, a blanket with mom and siblings' scent, a puppy health chart (vaccines & dewormer records), and a signed copy of the health guarantee.
All dogs shed to some extent, but doodles are known for very minimal shedding. The curlier the coat, the less shedding. F1 doodles work well for mild allergies; F1b and F1bb are better for moderate allergies. We can advise on each puppy's likely coat type around 4 weeks.
A $200–$400 nonrefundable but transferable deposit is accepted via Zelle, CashApp, or Cash. The remaining balance is due in CASH ONLY at pickup. For transported puppies, full payment is required 2–3 business days before departure via CashApp or Zelle. Contact Brooke at (260) 443-9035.
Yes! All visits are by appointment only, limited to 30 minutes. Puppies must be at least 6 weeks old. Please submit a completed application before your visit. We do not allow visits with multiple litters — choose 2–3 puppies to visit. Out-of-state families are welcome to FaceTime with us!
Puppies are not microchipped — we leave that to you and your vet. Most doodles can be registered through the Continental Kennel Club (CKC). All sires and dams are genetic health tested — documentation available on request.
